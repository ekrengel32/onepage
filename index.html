HELLO

I am a student of design and architecture living in Chicago, but I consider myself a mindful observer. A Texan Jew, my family comes from Algeria, Morocco, Israel, Czech Republic, and Poland. Growing up in a family with varying cultures and backgrounds is a piece of my foundation as an observer. I juggle divergent opinions and value systems inherent in the traditions of significant people in my life, which provides me with an opportunity to view the world from multiple perspectives. What began as a necessity of mental and social survival in the family, openness to people's opinions and value systems is now a practice that I cannot escape.

EXPERIENCE

I chose to study architecture at Illinois Institute of Technology (IIT) because of the rich architectural history associated with the school and the city of Chicago. I also believe the faculty offers a multitude of backgrounds from long-tenured professors to practicing architects who can provide different perspectives for my education. The facilities are condusive for aspiring designers, which includes a large workshop equipped with large and small tools that facilitate many design and build scenarios.

EDUCATION

I want to share my travels, designs, and interests while exposing myself with opportunities to build relationships with people. A public presence is critical to being found. I hope that this class will spark my ambitions of employing a digital interface that bridges these relationships. By the end of the semester, I want to confidently manage an environment that will serve as a personal portfolio of my life and work. Additionally, finish with enough knowledge and enthusiasm to continue learning about how to develop my content as my work evolves and grows.

CONTACT

When I finish school, I am open to continuing my studies by pursuing a master's degree abroad. However, my goal is to eventually pursue licensure as a professional architect. As an architect, I plan to establish a mindful approach that focuses on site specific and client oriented designs, while being open to any type of building use. I believe it is my responsibility to manipulate space and create environments for human engagement.
